Closure reports ( Logistics)

Shan Project

 

Logistics (Supply Chain Management, Technical & Operational Logistics) 

Challenges

Supply

-During the COVID pandemic, the entire town of Lashio was under lockdown, and most shops were closed, making it challenging for the procurement process to obtain the required supplies.The supply team was split and working remotely at that time, making it difficult to manage the workload due to stress. Some staff members were infected with COVID and could not come to the office, which increased the workload for the rest of the team.

- After the coup in Myanmar, we faced a shortage of goods, and prices dramatically increased and fluctuated. Since 2021, the border gate between China and Myanmar has been closed, resulting in a shortage of goods from China in the market. There was also a fuel shortage across all suppliers, with people queuing at fuel stations to get limited amounts of fuel. Additionally, due to the non-functioning banking system, the supply team faced significant challenges in following finance payment procedures. Most suppliers did not accept bank transfers or the K-Pay system. Consequently, the zero-cash policy was difficult to implement in the project.

- The medical pipeline items were significantly delayed in reaching the project due to MSF was unable to obtain the import permit. The items were transported via alternative routes, which resulted in further delays, and some short shelf-life items arrived with nearly expired dates.

-After the conflict escalated in the project area, government security forces restricted the movement of medical items across EAO territories. MSF cargo was confiscated at checkpoints at least once, affecting the transport of medical items from Lashio to the Muse clinic. Additionally, the shipment of reagents and equipment to Yangon via air transport was also restricted.

Technical

-Due to security constraints, biomedical equipment preventive maintenance companies have not visited the project since the conflict escalated in Northern Shan State. As a result, MSF terminated the service contracts with these companies.

- After the conflict escalated in the project area, project visits were restricted, and coordination support team visits nearly stopped, resulting in reduced support from coordination.

- After our main internet service provider exited Myanmar and sold their business to another company, the internet connection became unreliable, and services were not properly provided. As a result, frequent internet outages occurred, and some services could not be used in the project.

- The use of satellite phones and satellite internet devices is prohibited by the Myanmar government, with severe penalties for using them without a license. If the main telecom networks are down, it becomes challenging to use satellite phones.

Achievements 

 

-Since 2017, MSF has rehabilitated 10 MOH Decentralized Centers (DC) across Northern Shan State and donated the necessary furniture to improve health facilities for HIV patients. Additionally, MSF rehabilitated 2 ART centers and one TB ward in Northern Shan State.

-Over the last 10 years, MSF has constructed approximately 51 semi-permanent latrines and donated them to IDP camps in the Muse, NamKham, and Laukkaing areas.

-The project successfully responded to the urgent humanitarian needs in the IDP camps, providing medical care and non-food items (NFI) to those who fled from conflict-affected areas during the 2023 crisis.

-The project completed the construction of a clinic and office in Muse in 2017, setting up a facility that adhered to MSF standards, including specialized wards for HIV and TB care.

 

Major Construction/TechLog activities conducted

The Muse clinic and office, constructed in 2017, includes HIV consultation and TB wards. The total cost was approximately 100,000 EUR

 

 

CLOSURE/HANDOVER PROCESS 

 

-The project has retrieved valuable assets, including four vehicles and biomedical equipment, from the site and successfully sent them back to Yangon and the Muse location.

-MSF donated medical supplies from the main warehouse to Lashio General Hospital to support their healthcare services.
